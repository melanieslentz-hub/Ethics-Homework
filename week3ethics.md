Task: Find another example of the misuse of health data.
Answer:Another example of the misuse of health data was “The Untreated Syphilis study at Tuskegee.” which lasted 1932-1972. The way that the study was conducted was that the researchers took 600 African American men and separated them into two groups. The first group consisted of 399 men who had syphilis while the other 201 did not. However, the men with syphilis were not informed of their diagnosis, just simply told that they were being treated for “bad blood.” The actual purpose of the study was to observe the progression of untreated syphilis but these men were not aware of this. Even after it was discovered that penicillin was an effective cure the researchers still would not treat the men in order to continue collecting data.
Due to being left untreated for so long, many of the men developed severe health problems such as blindness, mental illness, and even death. The disease was also, unbeknownst by the men, passed on to spouses and children. In 1972, the study was eventually exposed leading to the end of the experiment  because of public outrage. Due to the participants being unaware both about their diagnosis and the nature of the study it left this vulnerable community to have a  damaging image of medical institutions.This misuse of health data could have been prevented in multiple ways, such as the researchers fully informing the participants and allowing them the right to withdraw. Along with this the study should have stopped once penicillin vaccine became available.

An example of bias in health data was the gender bias in cardiovascular health. Continuously, heart disease research focused almost exclusively on men, which resulted in diagnostic criteria, as well as treatment guidelines based significantly on man. Women were often never represented in these clinical trials, leading to their symptoms being labeled as “atypical.” 
As a result of this, women who were experiencing heart attacks were most likely to be misdiagnosed or diagnosed much later than men. The common symptoms that women experienced such as fatigue, nausea, shortness of breath, and back or jaw pain, were not reflected in health data leading to the consistent misdiagnoses. This specific bias has been a contributor to delayed treatment and poorer outcomes for women’s health, even with cardiovascular disease being a leading cause of death among women. A few ways that this bias could have been prevented would be by simply including more women in research for cardiovascular disease, analyzing data by sex, and updating clinical guidelines to recognize sex-specific symptoms.

Task: Find an example of anonymization of health data.
Answer :
An example of health data anonymization are the medical records released by the state of Massachusetts in the 1990s. These records were believed to be anonymized due to the dataset containing hospital visit records that had identifiers such as names and Social Security numbers removed. The specific anonymization technique used was data masking, which was where the personal identifiers were removed. 
Something that this case revealed is that by removing direct identifiers, it is still insufficient for protecting privacy. The technique overall failed because it did not address the risk of re-identification through data linkage. However, something that came from this case was, stronger anonymization methods such as k-anonymity, data generalization, such as broader age ranges or  ZIP code truncation, and different privacy methods were adopted more widely.

An example of a pro-forma consent document for health data collection is healthcare mergers and acquisitions. For example, when hospitals or other healthcare companies merge, patient data will be transferred or reviewed to make sure care and business operations continue.
